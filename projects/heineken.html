By project:

  1. Once I’ve joined the team, I started with doing a lot of reading - getting to know the brand, looking at both direct and indirect competition, studying the existing website, reading marketing materials aimed at the primary target audience. Finally - checking out the GA data for any patterns or abnormalities. The next step I proposed was facilitating a client workshop to get to the bottom of the current pain points, understand the scheme and learn more about the target audience’s needs and goals as seen by senior stakeholders at Heineken. We wanted to know as much as we could about retailers’ routine, their communication with Heineken reps and experience of using the current online platform. Saldy, hard deadlines would not allow for an extensive user research, but I felt some research is way better than no research. Our kick-off meeting (and the stakeholder workshop that followed after) resulted in quite a few important observations:

    For retailers their interaction and experience of Heineken was happening through TMs (they were effectively the primary - if not the only one - point of contact with Heineken). We wanted to investigate further. We defined “success” that we later one translated into some measurable goals and KPIs Our retailers were time poor, sceptical of Heineken (they believed that whatever Heineken were doing was purely for their own benefit), not very loyal (if a better offer would come along they’d have no problem switching to a competitor). There were “the experts” so any generic advice from a big brand would not go down very well. Heineken wanted to move away from the reward scheme centred approach and add more value (they wanted help with identifying more reasons for people to return to the website that just checking the voucher statement). Heineken wanted to attract new prospects BUT the right kind of prospects.
